# Communications Strategy — Overview

> Treat this as change management with a technical payload, not a technical rollout with an email attached. The gate is easy; getting ~85 teams to change how they work is the hard part.

## The failure mode we're insuring against
Teams feel ambushed → escalations land on the head of platforms → the gate gets switched off under pressure → the program dies. Every choice below exists to avoid triggering this chain.

## Audiences
One line each on who they are and what they need:
- **Executive sponsor** — air cover; gives the program authority.
- **Engineering managers / team leads** — own backlog priority; turn remediation into sprint work.
- **App-dev teams (API owners)** — do the actual remediation; need specific, actionable asks.
- **Leadership (progress audience)** — see the CIO-facing progress view; keeps funding and pressure healthy.

## Channels & formats
- Email — primary for phase announcements.
- Teams channel — support, office hours, lightweight reminders.
- Confluence governance hub — single source of truth; every message links here, never attaches.
- Per-team reports — the personalized "mirror" for the visibility phase.

## Cadence principle
A predictable drumbeat earns more compliance than volume. Pre-announce the rhythm; don't spam.

## Cross-cutting content principles (apply to every message)
- Lead with *why*, framed around their outcomes, not mandate.
- Name **new APIs vs existing APIs** separately in every message.
- Always link the self-service check and the playbook — reduce friction to act.
- Be honest about the cost; show how you've minimized it (pattern fixes, grace period, waivers).
- Differentiate audience: leadership gets risk/outcomes/progress; teams get specific/actionable.
- Always state the concrete ask, the specific violations, and the date.
