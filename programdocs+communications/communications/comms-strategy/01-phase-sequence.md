# Communication Phases

> Six phases. Each uses the same sub-structure. Don't advance until the success signal is met. Each phase names new-vs-existing separately and links the hub.

## Phase 0 — Pre-brief (internal air cover)
- **Timing / trigger:** before any external comm; after the toolkit exists.
- **Audience:** executive sponsor first, then eng managers / team leads.
- **Goal:** secure sponsorship and brief the manager layer before their developers hear about it.
- **Key message:** what's coming, why, the timeline, and the ask of *them* (prioritize remediation in backlogs).
- **Fundamentals carried:** the program's purpose, scope boundaries, the new-vs-existing split.
- **Docs linked:** Overview, Charter, Principles, Timeline.
- **Channel:** direct briefing / meeting + follow-up note.
- **Success signal:** sponsor visibly endorses; managers acknowledge and agree to prioritize.

## Phase 1 — Announcement (the why + the model)
- **Timing / trigger:** sponsorship secured; managers briefed.
- **Audience:** all app-dev teams (managers cc'd).
- **Goal:** introduce the program; establish understanding before any action.
- **Key message:** the program, the standard, benefits framed around their outcomes, the full timeline stated up front. No blocking yet.
- **Fundamentals carried:** what governance is and is NOT; how it works end to end (the model); the guiding principles.
- **Docs linked:** Overview, Glossary, How Governance Works, Principles, Charter, Timeline.
- **Channel:** email from/endorsed by the sponsor.
- **Success signal:** teams know the program exists and the timeline; low panic, questions flow to the support channel not to escalation.

## Phase 2 — Visibility (the mirror)
- **Timing / trigger:** rules turned on in report-only.
- **Audience:** each team, individually.
- **Goal:** show each team exactly where it stands; let them absorb their own gap. Lets you read the remediation curve.
- **Key message:** "rules are on in report-only — nothing blocks. Here's your current-state report and how to fix."
- **Docs linked:** Rules Catalogue, Validation Guide, Definition of Done, Per-Team Report.
- **Channel:** per-team delivery (not broadcast).
- **Success signal:** teams open their reports; early violation counts start moving.

## Phase 3 — Supported remediation
- **Timing / trigger:** teams have seen their reports.
- **Audience:** all teams + a leadership progress view.
- **Goal:** help teams fix at scale; build momentum; surface and help the stuck long tail.
- **Key message:** office hours, the top-violations playbook, visible progress, recognition for early movers. Optional carrot (catalog/portal/badge for conformant APIs).
- **Docs linked:** Top-Violations Playbook, Support, FAQ.
- **Channel:** Teams + email; progress view to leadership.
- **Success signal:** the remediation curve shows a defensible slope; a gate date becomes evidence-based.

## Phase 4 — Enforcement warning
- **Timing / trigger:** remediation curve supports a credible gate date.
- **Audience:** all teams (managers cc'd).
- **Goal:** give an unambiguous, dated warning with a clear escape hatch.
- **Key message:** "gate goes live on X," escalating reminders (T-30 / T-14 / T-7 / T-1), waiver path prominent. New APIs already gated by now.
- **Docs linked:** Timeline, Waiver Process.
- **Channel:** email + Teams reminders.
- **Success signal:** remaining non-conformant APIs are either remediating fast or have filed waivers.

## Phase 5 — Enforcement live
- **Timing / trigger:** the warned date arrives.
- **Audience:** all teams.
- **Goal:** turn the gate on without breaking trust.
- **Key message:** gate is on (soft gate first for legacy if chosen); what to do if blocked; waiver path; support channel.
- **Docs linked:** Waiver Process, Support, Timeline.
- **Channel:** email + Teams.
- **Success signal:** blocks are handled via waiver/remediation, not escalation; gate stays on.
